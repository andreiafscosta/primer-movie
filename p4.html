Why do you wanna travel in time?

Good old answer: making money!
In the stock market.

The pair develops a routine whereby they turn the machines on in the morning to warm up and then lock themselves in a hotel room and at the end of the day they research some stock picks that they can turn a profit on, but whose trading volume is significant enough to hide their actions.



With their picks in hand they go back to the beginning of the day and take profits on those stocks.



That’s it!



Of course things start to go wrong, not from paradoxes, but from ego and striving to control the device and others.



And as the film enters its final act, Abe and Aaron exit their boxes too many times, and start to suffer physical and psychological effects.

Doubles created via time travel are imperfect copies. This is the reason for Aaron and Abe's earbleeds and the degradation of their handwriting when they begin altering their past.

One final note.

The story has at least nine timelines and we only see scenes from seven of them.
